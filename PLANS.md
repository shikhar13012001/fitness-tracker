# Your Personal Training Plan & Protocol

## Your Profile

* **Height:** 5'10" (178 cm)
* **Weight:** 65 kg (\~143 lbs)
* **BMI:** 20.6 — healthy range, lower end
* **Body type:** Ectomorph (skinny / "hard gainer")
* **Goal:** Aesthetics, definition, visible muscle
* **Supplements available:** Whey protein, creatine

## The Hard Truth First

For your body type, **nutrition is the bigger lever than training**. Skinny guys usually don't fail to build muscle because their training sucks — they fail because they don't eat enough. You can follow the best program in the world and stay exactly 65 kg if your calories aren't there. Get this right before worrying about optimizing exercises.

Your play is a **lean bulk**: gain weight slowly (0.25–0.5 kg per week), mostly muscle, keeping body fat low so the "definition" you want actually shows up. At 65 kg with your frame, you have room to add 8–12 kg of quality mass before you'd need to cut.

## Rest Between Sets

* Compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift, rows, OHP): **2–3 minutes**
* Isolation lifts (curls, raises, extensions): **60–90 seconds**
* Abs/core: **45–60 seconds**

Your app's default rest timer should probably be 90s with easy 60s / 120s / 180s presets.


---

## Progression Rules

On compound lifts — if you hit all target reps with clean form, add 2.5 kg next session. If you fail the last set, repeat the same weight next session. Two failed sessions in a row → deload 10% and work back up.

On isolation lifts — add reps first (e.g. 10 → 11 → 12), then add weight once you hit the top of the rep range for all sets. Smaller jumps (1–2 kg).

Track every working set's weight × reps. The app we spec'd earlier will do the heavy lifting here.

## Nutrition Targets

Starting estimate (adjust based on weekly weigh-ins):

* **Calories:** \~2800–3000 / day (roughly 300–500 kcal above your likely maintenance of \~2500)
* **Protein:** 130 g/day (2 g per kg bodyweight) — non-negotiable
* **Carbs:** 350–400 g/day — fuel for training, especially important for your type
* **Fat:** 70–80 g/day
* **Water:** 3+ liters daily

**The adjustment rule:** weigh yourself once a week, same day, morning, after bathroom, before eating. If you're not up \~0.25–0.5 kg/week after 2–3 weeks, add 200 kcal/day and reassess. If you're gaining faster than 0.5 kg/week, cut 200 kcal — that's fat, not muscle.

Rough food framework: aim for 4 meals a day, each containing 30–35 g protein. One of those can be a whey shake.

## Supplement Protocol

### Creatine monohydrate — 5 g daily

Take any time of day — timing genuinely doesn't matter, consistency does. Mix into water, juice, or your protein shake. Skip the "loading phase" — just take 5 g every day, forever. You'll see slight water weight gain in the muscles in 2–4 weeks (this is a good thing — fuller, more aesthetic look). One of the most evidence-backed supplements that exists.

### Whey protein — 1–2 scoops daily

Use whey to *hit your protein target*, not as a meal replacement. If you eat 100 g of protein from food, one scoop (\~25 g) gets you to 125 g. If you ate less, have two scoops. Ideal timing is within 1–2 hours post-workout, or first thing in the morning on off days.

### Add these to your app's daily action items checklist:

* Creatine 5 g (anytime)
* Whey shake — post-workout (workout days)
* Whey shake — morning (rest days, if needed to hit protein)
* Water check-in (3L target)

## Warmup Protocol (every session)

5 minutes light cardio (rower, bike, or fast walk) — then dynamic stretching for the day's muscle groups — then warmup sets on the first compound lift: 50% × 8, 70% × 5, 85% × 3, then your working sets. Skip warmup sets on isolation work; just do one light set.

## Realistic Timeline

* **Months 1–3:** Newbie gains phase. Expect +3–5 kg bodyweight, strength on compounds could nearly double. Visible changes start around month 3.
* **Months 3–6:** Clear aesthetic changes — arms fill out, shoulders widen, chest develops. This is when people start saying "have you been working out?"
* **Months 6–12:** The "aesthetic" look you're chasing starts emerging — V-taper visible, defined arms, proper chest shape.
* **Year 2+:** Slower, more deliberate gains. You'll probably want to cycle between lean bulk and mini-cut phases to keep definition while adding mass.


---

## Red Flags — Back Off If...

* Strength going *down* for 2+ sessions on the same lift → you're under-recovered (undereating, undersleeping, or overtraining)
* Sharp or joint pain (not muscle burn) → stop the exercise, assess form, consider seeing a physio
* Constant fatigue, trouble sleeping, low motivation → deload week (half the volume, same intensity)
* Sick → rest fully, don't train through illness
